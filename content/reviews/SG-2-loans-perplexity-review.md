# SG-2 Car Loans (MAS LTV) — Perplexity Review (with citations)

## Factual accuracy
- LTV caps correct (70% for OMV ≤ S$20k; 60% for OMV > S$20k) — but stop framing as "Rules for 2026": these caps have been in place since MAS relaxed them in May 2016 [businesstimes.com].
- 7-year tenure cap is correct [sbr.com].
- "Most banks want at least 12 months remaining on your Employment Pass" is NOT a MAS rule. Soften this — lender requirements vary and public bank pages don't support a universal 12-month rule [uob.com].
- TDSR 55% is correct as a general Singapore lending rule but applies to housing loans and broader debt servicing, not a car-loan-specific reg [dbs.com].
- Loan amount is also constrained by income, credit, age, lender policy — not just LTV [dbs.com].
- Early redemption fee range S$250–S$500 is not supported by retrieved sources. Verify per lender before publishing.
- Interest rate ranges plausible but promotional and volatile. Rough confirmation: new cars ~2.78%, used ~2.98–3.38% [singsaver, moneysmart]. Flag with date and cited examples.

## Missing information
- Need to distinguish new car vs used car vs COE car — eligibility, down payment, tenure differ [paragonmotors].
- Some banks have foreigner-specific minimum income thresholds — worth mentioning [moneysmart].
- Local guarantor may be required by some lenders for non-residents [uob.com].
- Cash needed is NOT just the down payment: COE, GST, registration charges, insurance, PARF/ARF considerations [singsaver].
- Expand "what happens if expat leaves SG before loan ends" — refinance / sale / transfer options and documents for early redemption.

## Structural / editorial
- Split "Quick answer" into (i) MAS rules vs (ii) typical lender requirements so readers don't confuse regulation with bank policy.
- Rename "The LTV Rules" → "Current MAS Car Loan Limits" (avoid implying 2026 change).
- Cash Terms examples should label whether figures include COE, taxes, dealer fees; otherwise misleading.
- "Why Expats Get Rejected" blurs hard requirements with underwriting preferences — separate them.
- "Banks That Lend to Expats" is too promotional/generic. Either cite bank-specific criteria or replace with neutral note that policies vary.
- Reorder: MAS rules → total upfront cost → expat eligibility → documents → lender differences → interest rates → early settlement → FAQ.

- Example purchase prices risk being read as universal/current — SG car prices swing with COE.
- "MAS LTV still applies" for non-bank licensed moneylenders is overstated — moneylenders aren't the usual channel for car purchases.
- "No bank can be persuaded to exceed the caps" — broadly true for regulated banks, OK to keep but could be softened.
