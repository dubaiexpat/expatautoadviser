Reviewer stack: SG-1 via Gemini; SG-2/3/4/5 + HK-1/2/3/4/5 via Perplexity (for citations).
Date of review round: 2026-04-05.

## Hard factual errors caught (must-fix before publish)

1. **SG-3 Importing/Exporting — LTA transfer fee.** Draft said S$11. Correct current fee S$25. [LTA]
2. **SG-4 Family/Safety — Child-seat fine.** Draft said S$120. Correct S$150 composition fine, up to S$1,000 on conviction. [SPF/LTA]
3. **SG-4 Family/Safety — PHV exemption myth.** Draft implied private-hire vehicles exempt from child-restraint rule. They are NOT; only licensed taxis are exempt. [Road Traffic Act]
4. **HK-1 FRT — EV private-car concession cutoff.** Draft presented concessions (incl. One-for-One) as still live. They **expired 31 March 2026** for private EVs and were not extended. Commercial EVs, e-motorcycles, e-tricycles continue until 31 March 2028. [td, thestandard] — time-sensitive, highest-stakes correction in batch.
5. **SG-1 Buying as expat — LTV / rate figures** flagged as unverified against current MAS + bank postings; Patrick to re-verify at publish time.

## Structural rewrites required

- **SG-5 Subscription & Leasing.** Conflates subscription, long-term rental, and car-sharing (Car Club, BlueSG) as if interchangeable. Rewrite to separate the three models with distinct cost/commitment/use-case framing. No hard factual error but editorially misleading as-is.

## False flags from the reviewers (rejected)

- **HK-4 "used cars — no FRT" called misleading.** Incorrect flag. FRT is paid once at first HK registration; subsequent owners of used HK-registered cars do not pay it again. Kept as-is with clearer phrasing.
- **HK-4 alternative FRT tiers (40/75/100/115%).** Perplexity cited outdated source "hkstay". Current HK Transport Dept schedule = 46/86/115/132%. Kept original.

## Softening passes across the batch

Common pattern: draft language too definite where reality is insurer-/district-/case-dependent. Fixes applied across most articles:

- HK-2 Insurance: premium ranges, NCD-drop-on-claim, cross-border China pricing, overseas NCD transfer — all reframed as insurer-dependent examples rather than universal rules.
- HK-4 Parking: "no financial sense" softened to decision-rule framing (location/park/commute); parking ranges labelled illustrative with a government-parking anchor (HK$4,850 City Hall).
- HK-1 FRT: "FRT can exceed sticker price" → "materially exceed pre-tax price"; import requirements reframed as checklist not statute.
- SG-2 Insurance / SG-4 Family: example costs labelled planning ranges, not benchmarks.

## Meaningful additions merged in

- HK-1: taxable-value-vs-invoice-price clarification; commercial EV/e-motorcycle concession continuation note.
- HK-2: licence eligibility (HK licence / IDP), "how to compare policies" checklist, general-info disclaimer.
- HK-3: processing time 5–10 days (up to 20 with verification); IDP must be obtained pre-arrival; photo specs 40×50mm white bg; medical cert >70; eTD online renewal link; Poland/Greece NOT on Direct Issue List; DVLA evidence doesn't guarantee probation exemption (TD discretion).
- HK-4: building-parking bottleneck note; EV charging-access reality check; E-E-A-T author byline + verification date footnote.

- HK-4 citation: Cap. 374 Motor Vehicles (First Registration Tax) Regulation for FRT non-refundable statement.

## Patrick's net take

The review round paid for itself on HK-1 and SG-4 alone. Five hard factual fixes across the batch, one structural rewrite, one false-flag rejected, one outdated-source suggestion rejected. The rest was calibration — draft voice was too confident on numbers that are actually insurer- or district-dependent. Publishing this batch without review would have put two time-sensitive errors and two wrong-number errors in front of readers in the first week. Worthwhile exercise; worth repeating quarterly on any piece with regulatory or fee content.
