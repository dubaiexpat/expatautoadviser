# SG-3 Buying a Used Car (COE/PARF) — Perplexity Review

## 1) Factual accuracy
- "Every SG car has 10-year life from COE award" — imprecise. Vehicle usable period is tied to COE validity; the 10-year framing should be explained as the standard initial COE term, not a universal lifecycle rule [onemotoring.lta.gov].
- "After 10 years: scrap, export, or renew (PQP)" — broadly right but needs nuance. Deregistration can produce COE and (if eligible) PARF rebates; don't imply renewal is a casual default [moneysmart].
- PARF explanation needs correction. PARF eligibility depends on LTA conditions: new SG-registered or imported used car meeting the age condition at registration, deregistered before 10 years old — not simply "if still on ORIGINAL COE" [onemotoring.lta.gov].
- "Renewed COE = PARF gone permanently" — effectively correct but phrase carefully. After COE renewal and deregistration, rebate treatment changes; readers should verify eligibility in OneMotoring [moneysmart].
- **Transfer fee is wrong. LTA current fee is S$25, NOT S$11.** Ownership must be transferred within 7 days [onemotoring.lta].
- Loan rules incomplete: MAS caps 70% LTV for OMV ≤ S$20k, 60% for > S$20k, max 7-year tenure; used-car tenure can also be constrained by vehicle age/remaining COE [sbr.com].
- Inspection cost S$150–S$250 is plausible but present as rough market range, not fixed [singsaver].

## 2) Missing information
- Tell readers to verify COE expiry, ownership history, financing status, vehicle details via LTA/OneMotoring — not just the listing [onemotoring.lta].
- Include the CASE SAFE checklist reference [case].
- Expand "real ownership cost": road tax, insurance, inspection, financing, transfer/renewal costs — not just depreciation.
- Clarify dealer protections: warranties vary by dealer, not guaranteed by law. Recommend CaseTrust-SVTA accredited dealers [onemotoring.lta].
- Add "who this is for" note — newcomers benefit from accredited dealers because paperwork/financing/transfer is simpler.

## 3) Structure / editorial
- Open decision-oriented: remaining COE, PARF eligibility, condition, financing, title transfer — then explain why they drive price.
- Separate "what determines price" from "how to buy safely". New structure: pricing basics → checklist → dealer vs private → financing → paperwork → traps → FAQ.
- Label price bands as illustrative examples, not market rules.
- Use "compare mileage with age and service records" instead of a fixed 12,000–15,000 km/year rule.

## 4) SEO
- Primary keywords: used car Singapore; buying a used car in Singapore; COE remaining; PARF rebate; used car paperwork Singapore; transfer ownership LTA; second-hand car Singapore; used car inspection Singapore.
- Suggested H2s: How Used Car Pricing Works → COE Remaining and Value → PARF vs COE Rebate → What to Check in a Listing → Dealer vs Private Sale → Inspection and Transfer Paperwork → Used Car Financing Rules → Common Mistakes to Avoid → FAQ for Expats.
- Internal links: COE renewal/PQP guide; PARF/COE rebate calculator; SAFE checklist explainer; car loan guide; road tax renewal; dealer vs private seller guide.

## 5) Misleading / risky
- "COE remaining tracks price almost linearly" — too strong. Price is affected by model, mileage, accident history, loanability, PARF/COE position.
- "Dealer safer than private" is fair but don't imply private sales are inherently unsafe — CASE recommends professional evaluation regardless of seller.
- "Outstanding loans on private-sale cars that buyer inherits" is too blunt. Real risk: buying vehicle still under financing or not properly transferred — buyer must ensure no encumbrances.
- "Import a used car? Stick with SG market" — label as editorial recommendation.
- "Export when leaving? HK, Malaysia, others" — too vague. Say options exist, check destination-country import rules.

## Suggested opener (Perplexity's proposal)
"Buying a used car in Singapore comes down to four things: remaining COE, PARF eligibility, mileage/condition, and whether the paperwork is clean. For expats, a dealer sale is often simpler, but private sales can be fine if you verify the vehicle through LTA and get an independent inspection."
