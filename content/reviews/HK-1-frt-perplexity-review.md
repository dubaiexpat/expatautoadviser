# HK-1 First Registration Tax — Perplexity Review

## 1) Factual accuracy
- Basic definition correct: one-off tax on first registration of certain motor vehicles under Cap. 330 [budget].
- **BIG CORRECTION — EV concession timing:** As of **31 March 2026, general FRT concessions for electric PRIVATE cars (including the One-for-One Replacement Scheme) were NOT extended further**. Concessions for electric commercial vehicles, electric motorcycles, and electric motor tricycles continue until 31 March 2028 [thestandard, td].
- Draft says EV concessions still available in "early 2026" without noting the 1 April 2026 cutoff — outdated.
- "~HK$58,500 off FRT" only accurate for 1 April 2024 – 31 March 2026 period, NOT for applications on or after 1 April 2026 [info.gov].
- One-for-One cap ~HK$172,500 correct for the scheme period ending 31 March 2026 [kwiksure].
- Tax-rate breakdown may be correct but should be verified against official schedule — readers treat as statutory [budget].
- Imported-car section too absolute. HK import/approval rules more specific than "EURO 6 petrol, EV charging standard, RHD" [chinadailyhk].

## 2) Missing info
- Clarify FRT is charged on taxable value, not necessarily invoice price [budget].
- EV concession availability depends on vehicle type AND application date, especially after 1 April 2026 [td].
- Electric private car concessions ended, but electric commercial + 2/3-wheelers still have full waivers until 31 March 2028 [td].
- Note that used-overseas cars first registered in HK CAN still trigger FRT.
- Add paragraph on ownership cost beyond tax: parking, insurance, charging access, maintenance.

## 3) Structure / editorial
- Split "Quick answer" into short definition + "why it matters" sentence.
- Put rate structure before worked example.
- EV section needs date stamp: "as of 1 April 2026" or "for registrations before 1 April 2026" [td].
- Reframe "Importing own car" as "What expats should check before importing" with bullet list (approval, emissions, steering, charging compatibility).
- Label budget example as planning range, not factual benchmark.

## 5) Misleading / risky
- "FRT can exceed sticker price" soften to "can materially exceed the vehicle's pre-tax price".
- "Type approval (EURO 6 petrol, EV charging standard)" compresses several separate compliance issues — risky [epd].
- "Registered to you 6+ mo at origin, RHD" frame as practical checklist, not universal legal standard.
- Budget example shouldn't imply all expats need a mid-SUV.
