# HK-4 Buying a Car in HK (Parking Reality) — Perplexity Review

## 1) Factual accuracy
- **Parking data too specific without sourcing.** Government-managed Central parking can be as low as HK$4,850/month (City Hall, Star Ferry); some reserved options HK$6,200/month. Draft's "Central/Admiralty HK$6,000–12,000" plausible for premium private parking but too broad, not clearly private vs government [td].
- "No financial sense" too absolute. Better: "often poor value for central/island commuters, potentially worthwhile for families or outlying-district residents" [batgung].
- Perplexity quoted FRT tiers (40/75/100/115%) that differ from my HK-1 draft (46/86/115/132%). **My draft rates align with the HK Transport Dept current private-car schedule — Perplexity's "hkstay" source appears outdated. Do NOT adopt this change.**
- **Perplexity flagged "used (no FRT)" as misleading — this is wrong.** FRT is paid once at first registration; subsequent owners of used HK-registered cars do not pay it again. Keep as-is but phrase carefully: "FRT already paid by original buyer, no further FRT due on transfer".
- EV section needs updating — HK government EV roadmap targets 4,000 fast chargers by 2030, ~200,000 EVs. "EVs cheaper" too blunt without mentioning charging access + residual value risk [libertyinternational].
- HK$28 taxi flag-fall and HK$35–80k chauffeur figures need sourcing or removal.
- "15% of post-tax income" — present as editorial heuristic, not fact.
